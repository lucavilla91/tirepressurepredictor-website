FAQ

How do I get started?
Download the app below — fill in the form and you'll get started right away. A license key will be sent to your email after verification.
What data do I need?
Telemetry exports (ZIP or linked folder from WinTAX4, WinDarab, MoTeC). More sessions = better predictions.
How does the Run Filter work?
The Run Filter is an interactive scatter plot showing training runs by ambient temperature (x-axis) and best lap time (y-axis). Drag to select a region, or use step arrows for ±0.5°C and ±0.5s precision. Only selected runs feed into the prediction — exclude outlier sessions or match race-day conditions. Runs Summary Stats below the filter show per-run details, a laps histogram (green = enough laps, amber = fewer), and a warning when your target laps exceed available data.
How do temperature corrections work?
When current conditions differ from your training data, the predictor applies up to three corrections using the OptimumG gas law formula: (1) ambient temperature change (ΔT.Amb), (2) track surface temperature change (ΔT.Track), and (3) tire setup temperature — when tires are pressurized at a different temperature than ambient (T.Tires@Press). Each correction shows per-wheel pressure deltas. A color-coded bar indicates how large the temperature difference is.
How does the bleed correction work?
The bleed correction uses an exponential pressure build-up model fitted to your telemetry. Per-wheel expansion ratios (R = P_hot / P_cold) are computed from real data. Set race laps and formation laps — the system calculates R at the formation lap point and the bleed needed per wheel. Three modes: Constant Bleed (set desired bleed, get start pressure), Custom Start Pressure (set grid pressures, get expected bleed), and Grid Pressure (enter measured pressures from a practice grid simulation).
Can I transfer pressure data between tracks?
Yes. The Track Relations Matrix generates cross-track coefficients that let you transfer pressure knowledge between circuits. It uses bilinear interpolation within the data range and linear extrapolation outside it, providing reliable starting points even for tracks with limited data.
Does it work for long runs vs qualifying?
Yes. Training runs (qualifying, practice) feed the ML model for cold pressure prediction. Long runs and race stints are handled by Tire Pressure Logs, which tracks stabilized pressures and calculates K-factors per wheel.
What is Plateau Detection?
Plateau Detection automatically identifies when tire pressures have stabilized during a run (reached 97% of total build-up). These stabilized runs are auto-saved to your Session Logs as verified baselines, giving you reliable reference data without manual work.
Can I use it without TPMS?
Yes. If you record pressures manually (pre-session cold, post-session hot), you can use the Tire Pressure Logs in the Session Logs tab to track compounds, calculate K-factors, and get cold pressure recommendations.
What is Tire Pressure Logs?
Tire Pressure Logs lets you track tire compounds, build ΔP vs ΔT charts with trend lines, and auto-calculate K-factor regression per wheel. It includes a pressure calculator with OptimumG gas law formula for hot/cold conversion. Free-text cells with autocomplete and autosave.
