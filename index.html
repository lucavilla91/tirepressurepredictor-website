Key Features

Race Tires Management

Track tire compounds with a configurable dropdown, visualize pressure build-up with ΔP vs ΔT and ΔP vs Laps charts, and auto-calculate K factors (thermal gain coefficients) via linear regression. Includes an OptimumG-based pressure calculator.

Auto-Detect Telemetry Format

Drop a ZIP archive and the app automatically identifies the telemetry software (WinTax4, WinDarab, MoTeC) by sampling file contents with majority vote. No manual configuration needed — just upload and process.

Prediction History & Verification

Save every prediction with driver, event, session, and tire condition metadata. Filter by track, driver, or date. After the session, add actual hot pressures and verify predictions against real-world results with color-coded accuracy feedback.

Plateau Detection

Automatically detects when tire pressures stabilize during a run (97% of total build-up reached). Stabilized runs are auto-saved to your Prediction History as verified baselines — reliable reference data without any manual work.

Track Relations Matrix

Generate cross-track coefficients that transfer pressure knowledge between circuits. Uses bilinear interpolation within the data range and linear extrapolation outside it, so you get reliable starting points even for tracks with limited data.

Runsheet Editor

Interactive editor for manual pressure data entry. Import Excel files, edit cells inline, manage track/event/driver lists, auto-save with undo/redo, and export back to Excel. Works for teams without TPMS telemetry.

Pressure Unit Toggle

Switch between bar and PSI with a single click. All pressures across the app convert instantly — inputs, outputs, charts, and history. Internal calculations stay in bar for precision.

GPS Track Layout

Automatic GPS track map from telemetry data with numbered corner markers, start/finish flag, and track statistics (length, corners, lap time). Visual reference for track-specific analysis.

FAQ

What data do I need to start?
Telemetry exports (ZIP from WinTAX4, MoTeC, etc.) or manual Excel entries with hot/cold pressures, track name, and lap count. More sessions = better predictions.
Can I use it without TPMS?
Yes. If you record pressures manually (pre-session cold, post-session hot), the Excel workflow works perfectly. TPMS just automates data collection.
Does it work for long runs vs qualifying?
Yes. The model distinguishes session types. Long runs and qualifying have different thermal profiles, and the app accounts for this when predicting.
How do I get started?
Request a demo below. We'll walk you through setup, data import, and your first prediction.
What is Race Tires Management?
Race Tires Management lets you track tire compounds, visualize pressure build-up with ΔP vs ΔT and ΔP vs Laps charts, and automatically calculate K factors (thermal gain coefficients) via linear regression. It includes an OptimumG-based pressure calculator for converting between hot and cold pressures using the ideal gas law.
What is Plateau Detection?
Plateau Detection automatically identifies when tire pressures have stabilized during a run (reached 97% of total build-up). These stabilized runs are auto-saved to your Prediction History as verified baselines, giving you reliable reference data without manual work.
Can I transfer pressure data between tracks?
Yes. The Track Relations Matrix generates cross-track coefficients that let you transfer pressure knowledge between circuits. It uses bilinear interpolation within the data range and linear extrapolation outside it, providing reliable starting points even for tracks with limited data.
